Analogy: Using AI in Development Work

Imagine planning your wedding. A documentation team offers a package worth ₱40,000. They will use an iPhone 16 Pro Max to cover the event and CapCut for the same-day edit. The team only has 2 years of experience but has an above-average portfolio. Would you take the deal?

Most people would check their skills, not the tools. Even if they're using simple equipment, what matters is:

  • Their timing
  • Their ability to tell a story
  • The quality of their videos
  • The consistency of their work
  • And their professionalism

If their portfolio proves they can deliver beautiful results, then their tools do not determine their worth, it's their skill that matters.

This analogy helps explain the rise of “vibe coders” in the tech industry. These are people who rely heavily on AI tools like Copilot or ChatGPT to write code for them. While they can produce working output, the question is whether they truly understand what they’re building.

A real developer is like a real wedding videographer:

  • They know why things work.
  • They can fix issues without AI.
  • They can adapt when something goes wrong.
  • Their creativity and logic guide the project.

AI can be a helpful tool, just like an iPhone can be enough for a skilled videographer. But integrity, creativity, and true talent come from the person, not the tool.
