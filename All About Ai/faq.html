FAQs

1. What is AI?

AI stands for Artificial Intelligence. It is a technology that allows machines to perform tasks that usually require human thinking, such as recognizing patterns, generating text, or making predictions.

2. Is AI allowed in school?

Yes, but with responsible use. Schools usually allow AI for brainstorming, learning, and practice, but not for cheating or copying answers directly.

3. How do I avoid plagiarism when using AI?

Rewrite the AI's output using your own words, add your own understanding, and cite AI when required.

4. Can AI make mistakes?

Yes. AI can provide inaccurate, outdated, or incomplete answers. Users must verify facts before using them.

5. How do I cite AI?

Schools normally follow APA.

6. Does AI replace human skills?

No. AI assists with ideas and tasks, but human creativity, judgment, and understanding still matter. Still, it is better to be mindful of using Ai in our everday life.

7. Is AI biased?

Sometimes. AI learns from large datasets that may include bias. Always review AI output critically.

8. Can I use AI for coding?

Yes, but make sure you understand the code. Do not rely on AI for everything, especially if you cannot explain your work.

9. Is AI safe to use?

Yes, as long as you avoid sharing private information and stay within ethical guidelines.

10. How can AI help with schoolwork?

Ai can:

  • Explain lessons
  • summarize text
  • help with grammar
  • guide brainstorming

But you must always add your own ideas.
