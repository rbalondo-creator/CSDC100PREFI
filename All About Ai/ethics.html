ETHICS

1. Ethical Use of AI

AI should be used as a tool to support learning, not to replace critical thinking. Ethical use means being honest about how AI helped you, using it respectfully, and avoiding harmful or deceptive uses.

2. Responsible Use in the Academe

In schools, AI can help explain lessons, check grammar, and assist with brainstorming. But the student must still do their own understanding, analysis, and final writing. Responsible use includes following school rules and ensuring work remains original.

3. Avoiding Plagiarism and Misuse

Submitting AI-generated answers as your own without citation is plagiarism. Misuse includes cheating, producing work with no personal effort, generating harmful content, or copying AI output exactly. Students must rewrite, revise, and reflect on AI answers before using them.

4. Accuracy and Verification of AI Outputs

AI is not always correct. It may produce outdated, incomplete, or inaccurate information. Always verify facts using textbooks, credible websites, or official sources before claiming something is true.

5. Bias and Fairness

AI may produce content with unintentional bias due to training data. Users must check whether answers are balanced, respectful, and fair. Responsible use means not spreading discriminatory or harmful content generated by AI.

6. Recommended Guidelines for Using AI

  • Use AI as a learning tool, not as a shortcut.
  • Always rewrite and add your own understanding.
  • Verify all facts from reliable sources.
  • Cite AI when required by your school or teacher.
  • Do not input private or sensitive personal information.
  • Be critical—read and check if the AI answer makes sense.
  • Never use AI to create harmful, offensive, or misleading content.
